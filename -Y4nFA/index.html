Dr. Habiba Mohamud

Somali-American Physician and Public Health Expert
  • info@ugandakwekwaffe.com

  • Biography

    Dr. Habiba Mohamud is a prominent Somali-American physician and public health expert. She is currently the Director of the Center for Global Health at the University of Minnesota, where she leads a team of researchers and practitioners working to improve health outcomes in low-resource settings.

    Dr. Mohamud has extensive experience working in international public health, particularly in the areas of maternal and child health, nutrition, and infectious diseases. She has conducted research and implemented programs in countries such as Somalia, Kenya, Ethiopia, and Uganda. Her work has focused on improving access to healthcare services, reducing health disparities, and promoting healthy behaviors.

    Dr. Mohamud is a highly respected figure in the field of public health and has received numerous awards and recognitions for her work. She is a Fellow of the American College of Physicians and the American Public Health Association. She has also been recognized by the United Nations for her contributions to global health.

    In addition to her work in academia, Dr. Mohamud is also a strong advocate for health equity and social justice. She has spoken out about the need for increased investment in public health, particularly in low-income communities. She has also called for greater attention to the role of structural factors, such as poverty and discrimination, in shaping health outcomes.

    Dr. Habiba Mohamud is an inspiring leader and a passionate advocate for public health. Her work has made a significant impact on the lives of people around the world, and she continues to be a driving force for change in the field of global health.
